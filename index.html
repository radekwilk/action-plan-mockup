Make your actions S M A R T

S
specific

Clearly define the goal with the precise details

specific

Clearly define the goal with the precise details. A specific goal eliminates ambiguity and answers the question:

  • What exactly do I want to achieve?
  • Who is involved in this goal?
  • Where will this happen?
  • Why is this goal important?
X
M
measurable

It's important to know how to measure SMART goals.

measurable

It's important to know how to measure SMART goals. Goal tracking is a vital part of the goal-setting process

  • What metrics will I use to track this goal?
  • Who is involved in this goal?
  • Are there multiple ways to measure success for this goal?
  • What quantifiable change to the business am I hoping to achieve by accomplishing my goals?
  • How will I know when I have reached my goal?
X
A
Achievable

Ensure the GOAL is realistic but still challenging

Achievable

Ensure that the goal is realistic and achievable within the resources and constrains you have. This step ensures that the goal is challenging but possible to achieve:

  • Do I have the necessary resources, skills, and time to achieve this?
  • Are the any barriers that need to be addressed?
X
R
Relevant

Align the goal with your broader objectives

Relevant

Align the goal with your broader objectives. If your goal isn't directly connected to the work you're doing, it's probably not a good goal

  • Is this goal aligned with my personal or organizational objectives?
  • Does it contribute to company vision and long-term targets?
  • Will accomplishing this goal move my business forward?
X
T
Time-bound

Set a specific deadline or timeframe to complete the goal

Time-bound

Set a specific deadline or timeframe to complete the goal. Having a deadline creates urgency and motivates action

  • When will this goal be achieved?
  • What target dates should I meet to complete the goal?
  • What are the key milestones along the way?
